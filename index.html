School Profile

A day in the Life

Open Days

1978

Establishing the first stepping stone into the field of education, Century Tagore School envisioned to develop quality educational standards in Delhi. First private school set up in the locality.

1979

The school is established as a primary school. Pictures were taken on the 15th of August 1979 showing student's enthusiasm towards their heritage.

1980-82

Our first batch of students received appreciation from community leaders for the growing standards of education in the school.

1983

With our sincere commitment towards introducing quality education, the school soon gained popularity and within a few years the student population grew to 800.

1984-86

A proposal to establish a Senior Secondary School was approved.

1987

Establishment and foundation of Lancers Convent, a Senior Secondary School affiliated to CBSE. Lancers Convent was one of the first CBSE affiliated schools to start its operation in the 1980s.

1988

The Annual Celebration of the year 1988 was graced by the Chief Minister of Delhi.

1989

Lancers Convent won a prize in 'On the Spot Poetry Competition organized by Hindi Rashtriya Sangh.

1990-92

With over 3,000 students and over 100 teachers, the school has proven its quality education. Lancers became one of the most desirable schools in the region.

1993-95

The school's Eco Club received the Indira Award, a national award constituted by the Centre for Science and Environment, Delhi.

1996-99

School received a State Level Citation from the Ministry of Education for the model paper organized by the National Foundation of Indian Engineers; certificate by the Indian Red Cross.

2000

Two students at the school received internships at NASA. The school community supported affected families of the Kargil War.

2001

Lancerians received 3 Gold Medals at the District Level Delhi Talent Search meet. Prize in the Radio Quiz, broadcasted by All India Radio (FM).

2002-05

Spardhan Shree Award by the All India Cultural Society. Lancerians received 'Young Reporters' by TOI. Lancers Startup Prashant Vihar opened for the community.

2006

Recognized and awarded by Sahitya Academy for outstanding performance in academics.

2007-08

Lancerians set a National Record by winning Gold Medal in the shot put category. The school's basketball team won the state level championship.

2009

To establish a world-class quality education and to further expand the growth of Lancers, Lancers International School (An IB World School) was founded.

2010-13

Lancers received accreditation from Cambridge International Examination AS level /A level / IGCSE. Lancers offered the IB Diploma and the Primary Years Programmes to its school.

2013

Paras Sharma of Lancer Convent topped all over India with 99% in the Commerce. Lancers Start Up, DLF 5 and Sohna Road branches started their first academic year.

2015

Lancers students received 8 Gold Medals in the International Olympiads. 2 Gold Medals in boxing, District and State/National sub-urban levels received by Lancerians

2016

Lancers began offering the IB MYP, making it one of the first schools offering the IB Continuum of International Education in North India. The school was listed as a UN Chapter School.

2017

Our Lancers Community continues to grow to deliver quality education to the local community and the world.

DIVERSE STUDENT BODY

PERSONALISED LEARNING

Our faculty members personalise their teaching to each child’s learning style. They apply best practices to create classroom conditions that help increase attentiveness and information retention.

SMALL CLASS SIZES

Not only do students learn more in small classes, they also learn faster.

WIDE RANGE OF SPORTS

With a choice of over 25 sports, world-class facilities and coaches, and a chance to regularly compete in inter-school matches, students are kept healthy and active throughout their stay at Lancers.

COMMUNITY VOICES

THE LIS FAMILY

Sandro Skronias
Head Boy 2019-20

When I arrived in India and Lancers, I first was shy, scared and undisciplined but also eager to gain knowledge in this new environment. Lancers has not only taught me how to remember but also how to critically think and solve problems. It gave me many new obstacles and barriers to overcome, developing me as a pupil and as a person. It made me a potential and careful risk-taker. With this newfound knowledge, I decided to not only help myself but to help the community of Lancers as a whole. I see myself as principled, responsible, caring, open-minded, balanced and finally an inquirer. I believe the most important thing is to never stop asking questions as they will always lead to improvement.

Ananya Mahi
Head Girl 2019-20

I shall always hold pride and dignity to be identified as a ‘Lancerian’ for years to come. My short tenure, of two years, at this esteemed institution has bestowed upon me the fondest of memories, valuable life lessons and pertinent knowledge of the world for tackling life as I am to leave soon. The past two years have been the embodiment of sheer growth and maturation for me. Had I not been admitted to the school, I would never have discerned the significance of stepping out of one's comfort zone. In the words of Leslie Odom Jr. "I am addicted to growth" , and I thank Lancers for providing me and all my fellow Lancerians with an environment, this nourishing, to make that possible.
